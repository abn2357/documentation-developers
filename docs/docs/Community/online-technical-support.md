# Community Mission

The TRON community is a decentralized global collaborative network consisting of thousands of TRON ecosystem developers, blockchain technology experts, designers, end users, cryptocurrency holders and Web3 enthusiasts.

The mission of the TRON community is:

* **Promote the construction of an open Internet**: Committed to eliminating the barriers of centralized platforms and enabling developers and users to achieve true data ownership and content sovereignty through blockchain technology.
* **Support the sustainable development of the developer ecosystem**: Provide technical documentation, tool components, incentive mechanisms, and community support to help developers quickly start, deploy and operate TRON-based DApps and services.
* **Advocate community autonomy and co-construction**: Through the super representative governance mechanism and open voting system, encourage global community members to participate in network decision-making and ecological evolution.

The TRON community is not only a technical support network, but also a global collaborative platform to promote the popularization and innovation of blockchain. **Here, every participant is not a bystander, but a co-builder who shapes the future TRON network.**

# Participate in the community

## Code Contribution

The entire core code of the TRON protocol has been fully open-sourced on [GitHub](https://github.com/tronprotocol), and anyone can freely access, reuse, and contribute. We sincerely invite developers, researchers, and ecosystem partners around the world to participate in the continuous evolution of TRON and jointly build a stronger and more open decentralized network.

To learn how to participate in code contribution, please refer to the following guide: [How to contribute code](https://github.com/tronprotocol/java-tron/blob/develop/CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Proposal Governance

TRON's development path and technical governance are driven by the **TIP proposal mechanism**. Any community member can write and submit proposals according to the [standard process of TIP](https://github.com/tronprotocol/tips?tab=readme-ov-file#to-submit-a-tip) to participate in the improvement and evolution of the protocol level.

You can also view the proposals currently under discussion on the TIP platform, express your opinions or join the construction of community consensus: [View open TIP proposals](https://github.com/tronprotocol/tips/issues).

## Core Devs Community Call

TRON's key technical decisions and ecological development issues are usually discussed in depth in the [Core Devs Community Call](https://github.com/tronprotocol/pm/tree/master/TRON%20Core%20Devs%20Meetings).\
The meeting is open to all community members, and everyone is welcome to participate in topic submission and discussion.\
